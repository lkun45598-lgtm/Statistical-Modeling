## Paper Writing Strategy

The upgraded paper should not read as "we drew several SST figures." It should read as a complete statistical modeling solution:

1. **Problem abstraction:** South China Sea warming is represented by two coupled statistical objects: average-state SST/SSTA change and extreme-event marine heatwave risk.
2. **Indicator construction:** Build a reproducible indicator system from daily OSTIA SST: monthly anomalies, long-term trend, MHW frequency, total days, duration, maximum intensity, cumulative intensity, and composite heat-risk index.
3. **Modeling layer:** Use trend models, nonparametric robustness checks, threshold sensitivity, subregional comparison, lag correlation, and standardized regression to answer different statistical questions.
4. **Evidence chain:** Show that conclusions hold at three scales: whole-region mean, spatial grid, and subregion.
5. **Interpretation:** Explain that local wind speed anomaly is a stable statistical signal, while ENSO/IOD/PDO mainly provide weaker or lagged background modulation.
6. **Application:** Translate the model into monitoring and management recommendations for marine heat risk, not a speculative future forecast.
